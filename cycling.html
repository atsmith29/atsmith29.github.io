Cycling Life

Smith Family Cycling Tradition

Growing up, cycling was always part of our life. My father was an avid cyclist and cycle commuter. We would often take family cycling trips, including a trip to Moab, Utah, when I was 14. Growing up in Madison’s rich cycling community, and living on the Lake Monona bike loop, it was easy to fall in love with it.

Rebuilding, Riding, and Reconnecting

In my early adulthood, my friends and I would often fix up old bikes together. That DIY spirit stuck with me. In 2014, I got my Trek FX and began seriously cycling again.

During the pandemic, biking became a lifeline. My friends and I would take on long rides together; my personal record is 88 miles in a single day.

2025: Oceanus & New Goals

In 2025, I’ve been aiming to ride as often as possible. Alongside other lifestyle changes, cycling has helped me lose weight and improve my health.

For my 40th birthday, I gifted myself a Trek Checkpoint and named it Oceanus, as I was studying Greek literature audiobooks while breaking in the bike.
